Borage (Borago officinalis)

Next Sale: Spring Garden Fair & Plant Sale May 20th, 2018 (Ithaca High School)

Features Details
Type Annual (reseeds or collect seeds to use next year)
Zone
When it Blooms June-August
Where to Grow Full sun to partial shade
Soil Type Well draining, moist
When to divide Do not divide, long tap root. Soak peat pot until pot it is mushy and bury plant to top of peat pot and water. May flop in sun on first day but will recover the next day
Habit Height: 6” (24” with flower stalk), Spread: 6-12”, Clumping, occasionally reseeds
Scent Sweet
Attracts Butterflies, hummingbirds, and bees
Resists Deer and rabbits
Maturity Rate Flowers second year. Typically live 3-5 years, might reseed
Long Lived or Short Lived Long Lived
Something Special or Unique Leaves can be used fresh for tea giving the water a cucumber flavor-add lemon and chill for a replentishing summer drink. Companion plant for the vegetable garden-attracts bees and other beneficial insects. Flowers are sugary sweet and full of nectar especially in the morning.
