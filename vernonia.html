New York Ironweed (Vernonia noveborecensis)

Next Sale: Spring Garden Fair & Plant Sale May 20th, 2018 (Ithaca High School)

Features Details
Type Perennial
Zone 3-9
When it Blooms July
Where to Grow Part shade to full sun
Soil Type Any soil
When to divide Can be transplanted when less than 1 foot tall
Habit Height: 4-6’, Spread: 12”, Clumping, spreading by rosettes, but after many years
Scent Sweet
Attracts Bees and Butterflies
Resists Deer and rabbits
Maturity Rate Three years until full height, might bloom first year, but definitely second year. Slow to come up in spring, breaks ground early June
Long Lived or Short Lived Long lived; small first year, spends most of its energy setting roots
Something Special or Unique Very difficult to grow from seed. Prefers afternoon shade from sun. May sag in full heat of summer, but recovers in the evening. Monarch magnet
