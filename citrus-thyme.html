Citrus Thyme (Thymus citriodorus)

Next Sale: Spring Garden Fair & Plant Sale May 20th, 2018 (Ithaca High School)

Features Details
Type Edible perennial
Zone 5-10
When it Blooms June
Where to Grow Full sun
Soil Type Any soil, do not overwater (turns yellow)
When to divide Prefers not to be divided, easy to make cuttings an soak in water to start new plants
Habit Height: 6-8”, Spread: 8”, Clumping
Scent Citrus Thyme
Attracts Butterflies and bees
Resists Deer and rabbits
Maturity Rate
Long Lived or Short Lived Rejuvenate by starting new plants from cuttings, or by lifting plant sprigs and adding leafy compost underneath. Sprigs will grow new roots and rejuvenate plant
Something Special or Unique Excellent for chicken and beef or vegetable casseroles. Has a citrus aftertaste. Use three to four sprigs per recipe. Can also be used to make tea. Plant grown organically
